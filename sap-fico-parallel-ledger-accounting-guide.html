What is SAP FICO Parallel Ledger Accounting?

Parallel Ledger Accounting in SAP FICO allows businesses to maintain more than one ledger for different financial reporting standards. For example, a company may need to follow both local GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles) and international standards like IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards). With Parallel Ledger Accounting, businesses can post financial transactions in different ledgers simultaneously while keeping compliance with all standards.

Configuration of Parallel Ledger in SAP FICO

Define Ledgers

In the SAP system, companies can define multiple ledgers based on reporting needs. For example, one ledger could be for IFRS and another for local standards.

Assign Ledgers to Company Code

Once the ledgers are defined, they need to be assigned to the company code. This ensures transactions are recorded under the appropriate ledger.

Activate Parallel Accounting

Each ledger must be activated for parallel accounting. During activation, users assign specific accounting principles (like IFRS or local GAAP) to each ledger.

Adjustments and Customization

Additional settings may be configured to accommodate tax codes, currency conversions, or other special entries as required.

Benefits of Parallel Ledger Accounting in SAP FICO

Multi-Currency Support

Businesses can maintain ledgers in multiple currencies, making global operations easier.

Compliance with Standards

Allows businesses to adhere to both local and international accounting regulations.

Simplified Reconciliation

Simplifies the reconciliation process, ensuring consistency across different ledgers.

Flexible Reporting

Provides businesses with the flexibility to generate reports based on various accounting standards.
